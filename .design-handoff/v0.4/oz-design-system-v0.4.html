öz Design System

The visual + interaction system for öz — a peer-to-peer marketplace where Kazakhstanis in Korea exchange KZT ↔ KRW directly with each other, bank-to-bank. öz never holds funds. Trust is carried by receipts and ratings.

This document is the source of truth for any new öz surface — production, prototype, slide, or static mock. It supersedes earlier design-system docs (which still referenced an old internal codename, Saiqal; that name is removed everywhere in v0.4).

Mobile-first responsive web · Next.js 14 + Supabase Russian UI · English documentation Inter + JetBrains Mono

Rate widget

The aesthetic anchor of the product. A calm --primary @ 8% tinted surface (with r-xl 24px — one step bigger than cards) showing the live ₸ ▷ ₩ rate with an animated sparkline.

  1. 1
    Surface
    --rate-widget-bg = primary @ 8% on canvas. Border at 20%. Radius 24px (one step bigger than cards).
  2. 2
    Currency glyphs
    ₸ and ₩ set in Inter at 32px / 600 weight in --primary. NOT icons — type.
  3. 3
    Direction triangle
    Hollow outline right-pointing triangle, 20×20, 1.6px stroke, currentColor. Defined inline — sets the icon vocabulary for the rest of the product.
  4. 4
    Rate number
    JetBrains Mono 36px / 700, --primary, tabular-nums. Russian decimal comma (3,19).
  5. 5
    Sparkline
    Animated stroke-dasharray draws over 800ms ease-out on mount. Open-circle marker fades in at 800ms. Soft left-edge fade-mask so the line emerges rather than starting abruptly.
  6. 6
    Hover state
    transform: scale(1.01). One percent — imperceptible until compared. Read-only widget, minimal affordance.

Composites · new in v0.4

Slide-to-confirm

Drag the thumb to 85% of track width and release to fire confirm. Designed for destructive or trust-relevant confirmations — the counterparty's "I received the transfer" tap. Adds friction that protects against accidental confirms. Disabled = no interaction.

Always gate the slider behind an upstream check — a checkbox ("я сверил"), a step in a stepper, or a precondition like "receipt uploaded." On its own, the slider is just a fancy button. Gated, it becomes a real two-step confirmation pattern. See Verify gate → swipe.
.tx-slide   explorations/SlideToConfirm.jsx

Composites · new in v0.4

Press-and-hold

A button that requires a 2-second hold before firing. White fill animates left→right over the hold duration. Release early aborts + resets. Use when the user is declaring something already irreversible (e.g. "I sent the money from my bank app") — the hold signals "lock this in."

Hold duration thresholds. Default 2000ms for amounts > 500 000 ₸. For smaller transfers, 1200ms is sufficient — enough to defeat accidental taps without feeling slow.

Patterns

Receipt-as-evidence

The core trust mechanic. öz never holds funds. Two strangers swap currency through their own bank apps. The receipt screenshot is the only artifact the platform handles — and even the screenshot is custody of an image, not custody of money.

This shapes every transaction screen:

  • The sender screen shows the recipient's bank details with copy buttons, framed by a press-and-hold "Я отправил деньги" lock-in.
  • The upload screen captures the bank receipt screenshot. (Out of scope for v0.4 doc — handled by a standard image-upload sheet.)
  • The counterparty review screen (see Counterparty review) displays the receipt + gates the confirm action behind a checkbox ("я сверил в моём банке") and a swipe.
  • The wait state for the sender shows their own receipt captured back to them as reassurance.

The whole point: the receipt is evidence the counterparty visually verifies against their own bank app. The platform never claims to know whether money arrived.

Patterns · new in v0.4

User-submitted-receipt framing

When showing a receipt to the counterparty, the design MUST reframe it from "platform-verified evidence" to "user-submitted screenshot you must verify." This is critical to scam prevention.

Чек предоставлен контрагентом. Откройте банк, чтобы проверить поступление.

Rules.

  • Above every counterparty-visible receipt: a small italic line in --text-3 at 12px that includes both who provided the receipt and what the user is expected to do next.
  • Never use the word "подтверждено" or "проверено" near a user-submitted receipt. Those words are reserved for the counterparty's positive confirmation.
  • The submitted-receipt pill says чек получен (received), never проверен (verified). Verification is the user's job, in their bank app — not öz's.

Patterns · new in v0.4

Verify gate → swipe

Two-step pattern for confirming receipt of money. The user must (1) check a checkbox claiming they verified the deposit in their own bank, before (2) the slide-to-confirm becomes active. The gate isn't redundant — each step represents a distinct claim.

  1. 1
    "Откройте Kaspi и сверьте" deep-link row
    Logo + label + "Открыть ↗" pill — sends the user out to their bank app to actually check the deposit.
  2. 2
    Checkbox: "Я сверил поступление в моём банке"
    When checked, fills primary green. The slider's disabled state ties to this.
  3. 3
    Hint above slider
    When disabled: Отметьте «Я сверил», чтобы подтвердить получение. Dismissed when checkbox is on.
  4. 4
    Slide-to-confirm
    Active only after checkbox. The drag itself is the "I am committing this transaction" action.
Why both? The checkbox captures the claim ("I checked"). The swipe captures the commitment ("I'm releasing this"). Combining them is slower than a single tap, but for a transaction that releases money to a counterparty, the friction is the feature.

Patterns · new in v0.4

Press-and-hold for irreversible

On the sender's "Я отправил деньги" confirmation, a press-and-hold button replaces the tap-CTA. The user has already made the irreversible transfer in their bank app — the hold ergonomically matches the "lock this in" feeling.

Why hold instead of swipe? Different verbs for different moments. The counterparty's "I received it" is a claim about external reality — swipe reads as "I'm releasing this control." The sender's "I sent it" is a claim about an action they already took — hold reads as "yes, that was real, lock it in."

Patterns · new in v0.4

Patient waiting (no spinners)

Money is serious. öz uses no spinners, no progress bars without known durations, no animations under 800ms. When the user is waiting, the indicator tells them where they are and who's working on it, not "loading…"

  • Waiting pulse — 3 breathing dots at 1.6s. Used for "we're here" reassurance.
  • Progress stepper — 4 vertical stages. The active node has the same breathing-dot animation. The user can see what's done, what's next.
  • Live status text — "Контрагент просматривает чек" instead of "Loading…". Specific human action beats abstract system state.
  • Time-since-sent pill — calm green up to 5min, warm amber after that. Adds patience without alarm.

If the waiting stretches, surface a patience callout: a small warm-amber card explaining that banking delays sometimes happen and the user should not confirm receipt until money actually appears in their bank.

Patterns · new in v0.4

Mobile-first responsive

öz is a Next.js web app. Primary use case is Chrome / Safari on a phone. Secondary is a laptop browser. The same component code runs at both viewports.

Mobile (≤768px) — content fills the viewport edge-to-edge with the screen's canvas color (warm --bg for marketplace screens, --primary-soft for transaction-trust screens). No phone-chrome decoration in the actual web build — that lives only in the design-mock phone frame.

Desktop (≥1024px) — the canvas color extends across the full viewport. Content is constrained to a 480px max-width column centered on the canvas via a flex container with margin: 0 auto. Same cards, same hierarchy, same components — just centered with breathing room.

The transaction screens are intentionally narrow on desktop. They're focused single-task surfaces, not dashboards. Marketplace feed can widen on tablet, but transaction flows stay at the 480 column on every viewport.

Reference

Iconography

öz uses almost no icons. The product is type-led. When an icon appears, it's because no amount of typography would do the same job.

Used

  • Unicode currency: ₸ ₩ (type, not icons)
  • Hollow outline triangle (rate widget only)
  • Direction arrow: →
  • Star rating: ★ (gold)
  • Approx: ≈
  • Hairline lock (receipt-reveal)
  • The ö brand mark
  • Kaspi logo (third-party brand on bank-related surfaces only)

Not used

  • No icon font (Lucide / Heroicons / Phosphor)
  • No emoji
  • No country flags 🇰🇿 / 🇰🇷
  • No filled play / chevron / glyph cards
  • No left-bordered colored callouts
  • No spinners or confetti

If you need an icon — try harder to avoid it. If you must: hollow outline, 1.5–1.6px stroke, rounded line-caps, currentColor, 20×20 viewBox. The rate-widget triangle sets the vocabulary.

Reference

Voice + copy

Language. Russian product UI, English DS docs.

Tone. Calm. Competent. A little dry. No exclamation marks. No emoji. Confirmation is a quiet, complete sentence.

Person. Formal "вы" implicit in verb form. The system speaks as "мы" only sparingly ("Ожидаем отправки…", "Мы свяжемся с обеими сторонами…").

Buttons. Imperative single verb: Войти · Отправить · Подтвердить · Опубликовать · Связаться. Past-tense for already-done: Чек отправлен.

Loading. Gerund + ellipsis: Загружаем… · Проверяем… · Публикация…. Always …, never ....

Errors. Specific, blame-free: Неверный или истёкший код. · Не удалось загрузить фото. Попробуйте ещё раз.

Numbers. Russian decimal comma (3,19). Thin-space digit groups (750 000 ₸). Direction arrow is →, never ↔.

Six tonal touchstones

Вы отправляете первым— status, role-aware
Чек отправлен— perfective, no subject
Ожидаем отправки от контрагента— system as "we", sparingly
Загружаем…— gerund + ellipsis
Неверный или истёкший код.— error: specific, blame-free
Новый— new user, never "0 reviews"
